Before You Pitch

A few tips on
how to pitch.

You've built something. Now you need to sell it.
Here's how to not blow it.

How To Pitch

Keep it dead simple.

01 — Problem

What's broken?

Name the pain. Make it real. If there's no problem, there's no product.

02 — Money

How does it make money?

Turn the problem into a business. Show the money trail. Problem → product → revenue.

03 — Unique

What makes it special?

Why you? Why this? What do you have that no one else does?

04 — Why Now

Why would they miss out?

Create FOMO. What happens if they don't act? The train is leaving — are they on it or watching it go?

Don't overcomplicate. Story > slides. Pictures > walls of text.

How To Win

Commercial potential > technical complexity

Less talk. More signal. Build something people want.

01 — Problem

Does it pass the mum test?

Do customers clearly exist?

02 — Solution

Does it actually work?

Does the product solve the problem?

03 — Market

Big enough?

Venture-scale market with room to grow?

04 — Business Model

Show the money

How does the startup make money? Is it scalable?

05 — Moat

Why can't they copy you?

Tech, distribution, data, brand, speed?

06 — Product

Would people use this?

Working demo that feels real.
